Dubai - United Arab Emirates

Dubai is a city and an emirate in the United Arab Emirates. It is known for its modern architecture, luxury shopping, diverse culture and record-breaking attractions Some of the famous landmarks in Dubai are the Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world, the Palm Jumeirah, an artificial island shaped like a palm tree, and the Dubai Mall, the largest shopping mall in the world Dubai is also a popular destination for tourists, who can enjoy activities such as desert safaris, water parks, beaches and museums

The best time to visit Dubai depends on your preferences and budget. Generally, the winter months (November to February) are the most popular and pleasant time to visit Dubai, as the weather is mild and sunny, and there are many festivals and events happening However, this is also the peak season, so you can expect higher prices and crowds12 If you want to avoid the crowds and enjoy a quieter beach holiday, you may prefer the shoulder season (May, September and October), when the weather is still warm but not too hot, and the sea is comfortable for swimming2 You may also find better deals on accommodation and flights during this time. The summer months (June to August) are the hottest and most humid time to visit Dubai, with temperatures reaching up to 50°C (122°F) This is the low season, so you can find cheaper rates and fewer tourists, but you will also have to limit your outdoor activities and stay indoors most of the time The summer is also when Ramadan, the holy month of fasting for Muslims, usually occurs, which may affect some services and attractions

Dubai’s architecture is a reflection of its rich and diverse history, culture and vision. Dubai has a variety of architectural styles, ranging from traditional to modern, and from modest to extravagant Some of the examples of traditional architecture in Dubai are the wind towers, which are four-sided structures that capture the breeze and cool the buildings, the Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood, which is a preserved area of old houses and courtyards, and the Jumeirah Mosque, which is a beautiful example of Islamic architecture2 Dubai is also known for its modern and futuristic architecture, which showcases its ambition and innovation. Some of the most famous landmarks in Dubai are the Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world, which has a sleek and elegant design inspired by the desert flower Hymenocallis, the Palm Jumeirah, an artificial island shaped like a palm tree, which is a feat of engineering and creativity, and the Museum of the Future, which is a torus-shaped building with Arabic calligraphy on its facade Dubai’s architecture is constantly evolving and surprising the world with its originality and diversity1
